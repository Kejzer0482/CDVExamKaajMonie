A creative data visualisation exploring changing sea surface temperatures in the North Atlantic from 1850 to today.

This visualisation reimagines climate data as a memory archive ‚Äî one globe for each decade, representing over 170 years of warming oceans in the North Atlantic. Instead of lines and axes, we use planetary forms, glows, and rings to express shifting temperatures, growing variability, and the deepening impact of climate change.

The entire visual language is inspired by the Earth itself: the central globe represents our planet, the red ring evokes atmospheric instability, and the glow ring symbolises the broader climate impact ‚Äî an abstract aura of consequence. Together, they form a planetary system that reminds us: this data is not just about numbers ‚Äî it‚Äôs about the world we all live on.

How to read the visualisation

Earth icon: Represents average sea surface temperature (SST) for each decade. Bigger = warmer.

Glow ring: Symbolises abstract climate impact for the decade. Larger and brighter = more impactful.

Red ring: Indicates temperature variation (standard deviation). Bigger = more unstable.
